1. Provide a brief introduction to the WOD along with a link to this page for further details

Invoice 1 is the first new assignment type for creating a sales receipt. It asks the students to create a table listing 5 items, quantities, and prices. Students must also write HTML and Javascript to find the total by calculating subtotal and tax. All of the computing must be done dynamically and organized by comments.

2. Discuss your experiences in performing the WOD. What worked well, what did you stumble on or slowed you down.

On my first attempt, I struggled with how to limit the decimal calculations and place variables within a string. But after a few minutes of research, I learned `${}` allowed me to nest a variable inside a string. .toFixed(2) method rounds the string in two decimals.

3. Describe what you too did to prepare. What did you do similarly or differently from the WOD before this?

I’ve learned what works best for me is making the first attempt at the WOD until I fail to struggle and better learn the material being taught. Once I’ve fully completed the first attempt and satisfied with the time, I restart, hoping to complete the next time with an Rx score; this WOD was no different. It helped to quickly do a run-through of the code to refresh my memory on the correct code.

4. Discuss what you could have done to be better prepared and what you might try differently for the next WOD.

For next time I’d like to attempt to write a version of the WOD without the instructions and create the updates just by the mockup provided. Likely, I won’t get far, but I think getting into the mindset of starting from scratch is good practice.
